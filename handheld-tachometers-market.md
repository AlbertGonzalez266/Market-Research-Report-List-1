<p><ul><li>Non-Contact</li><li>Contact</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Handheld tachometers are devices used to measure rotational speed, and they primarily fall into two categories: contact and non-contact. Contact tachometers require physical contact with the rotating object, using a mechanical probe or wheel to gauge speed. In contrast, non-contact tachometers utilize laser or infrared technology to measure the speed remotely, making them suitable for delicate or hard-to-reach applications. Each type serves different industry needs, balancing precision and ease of use depending on specific measurement requirements.</p></p>
