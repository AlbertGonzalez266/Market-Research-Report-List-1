<p><p>The Sail and Rowing Dinghies market features various types, including coxless and coxed pairs. Coxless pairs consist of two rowers working in harmony without a coxswain to steer, focusing solely on their rowing technique and speed. In contrast, coxed pairs include a coxswain who guides the boat, offering steering and performance strategy while the rowers concentrate on their strokes. Both types cater to different preferences in competitive and recreational rowing, influencing participation and equipment demand in the market.</p></p>
