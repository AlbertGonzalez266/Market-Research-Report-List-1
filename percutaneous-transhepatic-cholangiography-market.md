<p><p>Percutaneous Transhepatic Cholangiography (PTC) is a medical imaging technique used to visualize the bile ducts and assess conditions such as obstructions, strictures, or lesions. It involves the insertion of a needle through the skin and into the liver to inject contrast dye into the bile ducts, allowing for X-ray imaging. This procedure is particularly valuable in cases where other imaging methods, such as MRI or ultrasound, fail to provide adequate information.</p><p>The PTC market is experiencing significant growth driven by the rising incidence of liver and biliary diseases, advancements in imaging technologies, and increasing demand for minimally invasive procedures.
